I'm currently a Software Development Engineer at Amazon, where my work spans the full stack—from architecting distributed systems at scale to building production-grade ML pipelines.

I've been deeply passionate about Machine Learning since high school. I've published research at NYU, Stanford, and UCSF on ML applications in medical imaging, including developing CNNs for prostate cancer detection and analyzing Alzheimer's disease biomarkers. Beyond my professional work, I'm deeply engaged in the AI research community—hosting Kaggle hackathons and continuously exploring cutting-edge architectures through personal projects, from GANs and RNNs to reinforcement learning systems.

Security is another interest of mine. I serve as a Security Guardian at Amazon, conducting threat modeling and security reviews to protect our systems. Previously, I spent two summers as an NSF Research Fellow at NYU's Cybersecurity Lab, where I built web scrapers and NLP systems to analyze dark web data and uncover security threats.
